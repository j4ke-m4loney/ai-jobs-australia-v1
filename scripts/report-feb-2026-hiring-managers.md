## 3. Salary: The Elephant in the Room

**Only 13.5% of AI job listings disclose salary.** That means 86.5% of employers are asking candidates to apply blind.

Here's why that's a problem: candidates are increasingly filtering and self-selecting based on salary expectations. If you're not disclosing, you're losing applicants to those who are.

## 4. The Remote Work Gap

This is the biggest mismatch in the data:

| | Supply (Jobs) | Demand (Searches) |
|--|--------------|-------------------|
| Remote | 9.6% | 24.2% |
| Hybrid | 55.6% | — |
| Onsite | 34.8% | — |

**24% of candidates specifically filter for remote work, but only 9.6% of jobs offer it.** If your role can be done remotely, advertising it as such immediately puts you in a less competitive pool.

Hybrid is the standard (56% of listings), so offering hybrid alone doesn't differentiate you. But offering **genuine remote flexibility** — especially for ML and Infrastructure roles — could be a significant talent magnet.

If you're hiring ML engineers and offering remote, you're aligned with the market. If you're hiring Data Scientists and requiring onsite, know that 58% of Data Science roles are onsite — you're competing with a large pool of similar listings.

## 8. The Opportunity for Employers

Based on this month's data, here are the highest-leverage moves for hiring managers:

### Disclose your salary
Only 13.5% of listings show salary. Candidates increasingly expect it, and listings with salary information convert better. You don't need to give an exact number — a range works.

### Offer remote or genuine hybrid
24% of candidates want remote, but only 10% of jobs offer it. Even partial remote flexibility puts you ahead of 90% of the market.

### Use searchable job titles
"AI Engineer", "Data Scientist", "Machine Learning Engineer" — these are what candidates type into the search bar. Don't bury the role behind corporate jargon.

### Don't overlook junior talent
"Junior" and "entry level" are in the top search terms, but only 8 internships and zero graduate programmes were posted this month. There's an undersupplied segment of eager candidates.

### Move fast
With 792 active listings and growing, candidates have choices. The average AI role is hard to interview for — if your process takes 6+ weeks, you're likely losing finalists to faster offers.
