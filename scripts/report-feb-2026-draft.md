# The State of AI Jobs in Australia — February 2026

*Published: February 2026 | AI Jobs Australia Monthly Report*

Australia's AI job market continues to accelerate into 2026. This is our first monthly report — a data-driven look at what's happening in AI, Machine Learning, and Data Science hiring across the country, drawn directly from real job listings and platform activity on AI Jobs Australia.

---

## Headline Numbers

In the first 17 days of February 2026:

- **178** new jobs approved (tracking ahead of January's 215 full-month total)

## What This Means

February's data tells a clear story: **the Australian AI job market is growing, and fast.**

1. **Apply clicks have more than doubled** month-over-month — job seekers are moving from browsing to action
2. **AI Engineer is the most sought-after title**, but there's growing interest in entry-level positions
3. **Hybrid work dominates supply (56%)**, but there's a remote work gap — 24% of searches filter for remote, only 10% of jobs offer it
4. **ML roles pay the most** at $191K midpoint, nearly $50K above the overall average
5. **Salary transparency is low** at just 13.5% — employers who disclose salary have a competitive advantage in attracting applicants
6. **Banks are the biggest hirers** — CommBank, Westpac, Suncorp, and Bankwest account for 10%+ of all listings
7. **AI chatbots are emerging as a discovery channel** — ChatGPT and Perplexity combined now refer more traffic than LinkedIn
8. **The interviews are tough** — 67% of roles are rated hard or very hard, reflecting the technical bar in AI/ML
